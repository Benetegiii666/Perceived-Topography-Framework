# SEED_001: Concept Architecture v0.1

> Original concept architecture from the OpenAI co-author sessions.

## Core Thesis

Behavior emerges from the interaction between optimizers and perceived topography.

## Supporting Theses

1. Topography exerts greater influence on behavior than incentives alone.
2. Perception mediates the relationship between topography and behavior.
3. Governance functions primarily through environmental design rather than direct behavioral control.
4. AI alignment concerns can be reframed as a special case of designing environments for optimization.

## Definitions

- **Reality:** The actual state of the world, independent of observation.
- **Topography:** The structure of gradients, affordances, constraints, costs, permissions, incentives, and visibility through which behavior becomes more or less likely to emerge.
- **Perception:** The optimizer's constructed model of reality and the topography visible from its position.
- **Optimization:** The process by which a system responds to perceived gradients in pursuit of an objective.
- **Behavior:** The observable pattern that emerges from the interaction between an optimizer and its perceived topography.
- **Outcomes:** Changes in reality produced by behavior.

## Hierarchy

```
Reality → Topography → Perception → Optimization → Interaction → Emergent Behavior → Outcomes → Reality
```

## Open Questions

- Is topography fundamental or derived?
- Is perception a layer or a mechanism?
- Is governance upstream of topography or synonymous with topography design?
