# CB_001: Claude Reduction Branch

## Purpose

Preserves Claude's reduction exercise without allowing it to replace the larger framework. The reduction was valuable but represents one analytical path, not settled architecture.

## Key Contributions

Claude pressure-tested the proposed organizational reasoning architecture and performed D011-style reduction on all entities.

### Survivors

- **Claim** proposed as the atomic unit of organizational reasoning (assertion + basis + boundary)
- **Working Theory** confirmed as useful (composite of Claims)
- **Uncertainty** identified as important and missing from original proposal
- **Contradiction** should be explicit and structural, not optional
- **Reasoning State** confirmed as a meaningful and distinct retrieval target

### Reduction Results

**Minimum viable objects:** Claim, Decision, Outcome
**Core cycle:** Believe → Decide → Observe → Compare → Revise
**Deepest principle:** Organizations learn only from explicit comparison of beliefs to outcomes

### Proposed Implementation

Decision journal with retrieval — agent-maintained journal of consequential decisions with Claims, Evidence, and Outcomes. Surfaced at point of next similar decision.

## Important Boundary

The decision journal is currently viewed as a **validation mechanism**, not final architecture. The reduction exercise was valuable. The resulting MVP should not be mistaken for the complete framework.

## Red Team Results

Claude red-teamed the reduction and identified:
1. Model/Working Theory may survive D011 as accumulated state (temporal primitive, not functional primitive)
2. "Evidence is the one thing to implement" may reflect engineering bias
3. Model Fragmentation, Temporal Decay, Premature Formalization, and Interpretive Drift as failure modes
4. Deliberate reasoning may not be the primary way organizations learn

## Relationship to Layer Separation (FL_010)

This reduction exercise operated primarily at the **Theory** and **Architecture** layers simultaneously — which contributed to the realization that those layers need to be kept separate. The reduction's theoretical conclusions (Claim as atomic unit) and architectural conclusions (Working Theory as useful entity) are valid at their respective layers.
