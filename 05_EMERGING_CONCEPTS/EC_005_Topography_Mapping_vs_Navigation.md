# EC_005: Topography Mapping vs Topography Navigation

**Status:** Pressure only — do not promote
**Date:** 2026-06-09

## Observation

Most framework components describe how optimizers **navigate** perceived topography — responding to gradients, constructing perception, selecting paths. The VP Marketing Visibility Interview operates differently. Its purpose is to **discover, reveal, and map** gradients, visibility gaps, and failure modes.

These may be fundamentally different activities.

## Potential Distinction

| Mode | Purpose | Operates On |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Navigation** | Act within a perceived topography | Perceived gradients |
| **Mapping** | Reveal the structure of the topography itself | The gap between reality and perception |

**Navigation systems** operate within a perceived topography. They optimize, select paths, respond to gradients.

**Mapping systems** attempt to reveal the structure of the topography itself — including what is invisible, misread, or ignored.

## Examples

### Navigation
- Marketing campaign planning
- Agent behavior (AI or human)
- Organizational decision-making
- Route selection, resource allocation

### Mapping
- VP Visibility Interview
- Diagnostic assessments
- Root-cause investigations
- Security audits, red team exercises
- Scientific experiments (designed to reveal what's hidden)

## Why This Might Matter

If mapping and navigation are genuinely distinct:
1. The framework needs to account for both — currently it primarily describes navigation
2. Mapping may be a **meta-activity** — the act of improving the information surface so that future navigation is better informed
3. The VP interview is a mapping tool that produces a better information surface for the consultant, who then navigates using it
4. Governance may operate primarily in mapping mode — revealing topography so that navigators can act on better information

## D011 Test Required

Per the Differential Intervention Principle: does distinguishing mapping from navigation produce meaningfully different predictions or interventions?

- If a team is **navigating poorly**, the intervention targets perception construction or optimization
- If a team is **mapping poorly**, the intervention targets the discovery process — how they identify gradients they don't yet know about

If those interventions are different and differentially effective, the distinction earns its place. If not, mapping may just be a subtype of navigation.
