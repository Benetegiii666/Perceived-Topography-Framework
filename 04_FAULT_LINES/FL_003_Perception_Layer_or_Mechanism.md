# FL_003: Is Perception a Layer or a Mechanism?

**Status:** Open — under active pressure from FL_006 and FL_007
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## The Tension

Is perception a *layer* in the hierarchy (a stage that inputs pass through) or a *mechanism* (an active process with its own dynamics)?

If a layer: perception is a filter between topography and optimization. It simplifies, distorts, or selects from available information. The model is: Topography → [perception filter] → Optimization.

If a mechanism: perception is an active construction process. It doesn't just filter — it builds. The model is: Topography → [perception construction process] → Optimization.

## Why It Matters

The "layer" framing suggests perception is passive and the main design lever is topography.
The "mechanism" framing suggests perception is active and can be designed independently of topography.

FL_006 and FL_007 are pushing hard toward "mechanism" — with Information Surfaces as the substrate and Perception Construction as the process.

## Current Direction

The seed material already used the word "constructed" in the definition of perception: "the optimizer's *constructed* model of reality." This suggests the seed authors intuited "mechanism" even while framing it as "layer." The current session has made this explicit.
