# FL_002: Is Topography Fundamental or Derived?

**Status:** Open
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## The Tension

Is "topography" a fundamental concept — a real structural feature of environments — or is it a derived metaphor that could be restated in terms of incentives, constraints, fields, or affordances?

If fundamental: topography is a genuine analytical category that adds explanatory power beyond existing frameworks.
If derived: topography is a useful visualization but adds no new insight beyond what incentive theory, game theory, or field theory already provide.

## Why It Matters

The entire framework rests on topography as a central concept. If it can be fully reduced to existing concepts (CA_002: "Topography Is Just Incentives"), the framework is a relabeling exercise, not a theoretical contribution.

## Evidence For Fundamental
- The framework appears to apply across domains where no single existing theory covers all cases
- Topography captures *structure* (gradients, valleys, ridges) that pure incentive models miss
- Information surfaces (EC_004) are hard to express purely in incentive terms

## Evidence For Derived
- Every topographic feature can arguably be restated as a set of costs, rewards, and constraints
- The marble metaphor is compelling but may not survive formalization
- No empirical data yet distinguishes topographic explanations from incentive-based ones
