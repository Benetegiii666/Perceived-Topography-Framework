# FL_005: Are Optimizers Choosing Paths or Responding to Gradients?

**Status:** Under exploration
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## The Tension

Two models of how agents interact with topography:

**Path model:** Agents perceive discrete options (paths) and choose among them. Behavior is a sequence of choices at forks. This implies deliberation, comparison, and selection.

**Gradient model:** Agents respond to local gradients (slopes) and flow toward lower-energy states. Behavior is continuous movement along the steepest descent. This implies automatic, non-deliberative response.

## Why It Matters

The marble metaphor (AHA_005) suggests the gradient model — marbles don't choose paths, they follow slopes. But human agents clearly do choose paths in many situations (career decisions, strategic planning).

The resolution may determine whether the framework is better suited to:
- High-frequency, low-deliberation behavior (gradient model)
- Low-frequency, high-deliberation behavior (path model)
- Or whether both are valid at different scales

## Connection to FL_001

This is related to FL_001 (Emergent or Chosen). The gradient model maps to emergence; the path model maps to choice. If FL_001 resolves as "both/and," this fault line may resolve similarly — with gradients dominating at scale and paths dominating at the individual decision level.
