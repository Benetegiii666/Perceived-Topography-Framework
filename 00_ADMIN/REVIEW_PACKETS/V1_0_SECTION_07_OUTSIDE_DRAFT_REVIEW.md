**Theoretical role of Discovery:**

The draft's opening two sentences establish the temporal distinction cleanly:

> "Learning changes reasoning after reality answers back. Discovery works earlier. It captures the human reasoning that should shape the landscape before the system acts."

This is stronger than v0.1/v0.2, which relied on positional context (after Reasoning Architecture) rather than explicit temporal framing.

**Constructive nature of Discovery:**

The draft explicitly addresses the continuity audit's concern that Discovery might sound like mere extraction:

> "Some of that reasoning is tacit. Some is contested. Some becomes clear only when a system proposes the wrong interpretation and the human sees what needs correction."

This preserves the constructive dimension — Discovery helps humans *form* reasoning, not merely extract it.

## Required Corrections Before Insertion

### 1. Restore the design principle formulation

- **Issue:** "Do the reasoning work before asking the user to do it" — the source packet's primary design principle and a protected formulation across all three generations — does not appear as a direct statement in the draft.
- **Location:** The draft says "The system should infer what it can from existing information surfaces, expose the inference, and ask humans to confirm only what matters." This is a paraphrase, not the formulation.
- **Why it matters:** This is the section's most memorable and citeable sentence. It has appeared in every generation. It is listed as protected language in the source-packet review. Its absence is a continuity gap.
- **Smallest sufficient correction:** Add the sentence (as a blockquote or inline emphasis) in the "Discovery runs on infer-confirm, not blank forms" subsection, near where the middle path is introduced. One sentence.
- **Authorial approval required:** No — this is a preservation, not a new formulation.

### 2. Restore the safety formulation

- **Issue:** "Discovery shapes the starting optimizer state. Because starting state shapes motion, discovery shapes safety." This protected formulation from v0.1/v0.2 and the source packet does not appear in the draft.
- **Location:** The draft's "Discovery makes policy and consequence visible before action" subsection covers the safety argument through examples but does not state the connection explicitly.
- **Why it matters:** This formulation links Discovery to the entire motion/sufficiency framework (S4). It is one of six protected formulations in the source packet. Its absence weakens the theoretical connection between Discovery and the rest of the framework.
- **Smallest sufficient correction:** Add one sentence in either the tool-action subsection or the closing "Discovery as governance" subsection. The exact formulation or a close paraphrase.
- **Authorial approval required:** No — this is a preservation.

### 3. Add Discovery Pattern Update mention

- **Issue:** Discovery Pattern Updates are not mentioned in the draft. The source packet says "brief mention in S7; detailed treatment in S8" and the concept was preserved across all three generations.
- **Location:** Not present anywhere in the draft.
- **Why it matters:** The idea that the Discovery process itself can learn and improve is a distinct conceptual contribution. Without even a brief mention, the reader reaches Section 8 without knowing the concept exists. The continuity audit flagged this as a pre-drafting requirement.
- **Smallest sufficient correction:** Add 1–2 sentences in the "Discovery as governance" subsection or near the closing. Example: "Discovery can also learn about itself. Over time, the system can identify which interaction patterns produce lower friction, better reasoning, and better outcomes. That adaptive capacity — captured as Discovery Pattern Updates — makes the process itself improve rather than remaining static."
- **Authorial approval required:** No — this is a preservation from v0.1/v0.2.
