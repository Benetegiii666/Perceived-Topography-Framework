# Reader Journey — Paper v0.1 Revision Pass

The reader journey should feel like a progressive reveal, not a glossary dump.

## The Intended Experience

1. The reader begins with the familiar fear of agentic AI.
2. The paper reframes the problem from agent morality to landscape design.
3. The reader learns the core vocabulary needed to avoid ambiguity.
4. The reader sees that context alone is not reasoning state.
5. The reader learns the core engine: optimizer, topography, motion.
6. The reader sees how hallucination and harmful action share premature sufficiency.
7. The reader learns that learning requires prediction error and model update.
8. The reader sees how learning becomes durable through Model Update Objects.
9. The reader sees how reasoning state persists through six core objects.
10. The reader sees how discovery converts messy intent into reasoning state without user burden.
11. The reader watches the full loop operate in a healthcare campaign example.
12. The reader sees how to implement the architecture incrementally.
13. The reader sees how to test and falsify it.
14. The reader sees the intellectual lineage.
15. The reader sees practical implications.
16. The reader returns to the central claim: agents are optimizers moving through landscapes, and better motion requires better landscapes.

## Revision Principle

Each section should make the next section feel necessary.

## Section Arcs

| Arc | Sections | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| Reframe | 1-3 | Fear → vocabulary → missing layer |
| Engine | 4-6 | Optimizer → topography → motion |
| Failure + Learning | 7-9 | Failure → learning → durable learning |
| Architecture + Discovery | 10-12 | Reasoning objects → discovery → running example |
| Buildable + Testable | 13-14 | Implementation → validation |
| Position + Close | 15-17 | Related work → implications → conclusion |

## Structural Risks

1. **Sections 11 and 12** may feel repetitive — Section 12 must demonstrate, not re-explain
2. **Sections 10 and 15** both touch knowledge management — 10 stays operational, 15 stays scholarly
3. **Section 14** must include assumption verification as a visible heading
