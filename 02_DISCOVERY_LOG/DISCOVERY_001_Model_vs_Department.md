# DISCOVERY_001: Model vs Department

**Status:** Open
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## Observation

The danger is not necessarily the model. It is the department.

When organizations deploy AI agents, the risk conversation focuses on model capabilities — what the model can do, what it might do unprompted, how to constrain it. But the larger risk often sits in the organizational structure around the model: who deploys it, what objectives they set, what information surfaces they expose to it, and what oversight exists.

## Implication

If the department is more dangerous than the model, then AI safety is partly an organizational design problem, not purely a technical one. The topography the organization creates around the agent matters more than the agent's raw capabilities.
