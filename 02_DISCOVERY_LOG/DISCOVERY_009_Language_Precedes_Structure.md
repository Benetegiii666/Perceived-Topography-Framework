# DISCOVERY_009: The Framework Appears in Language Before It Appears in Structure

**Status:** Open
**Date:** 2026-06-09

## Observation

The concept of perception construction existed in the original seed material as a descriptive adjective: "the optimizer's *constructed* model of reality." At the time, "constructed" was doing almost no work — it simply meant "perception isn't reality."

Subsequent fault lines (FL_003, FL_006, FL_007) revealed that "constructed" may represent a distinct mechanism within the framework. The adjective became an architectural layer.

## Implication

Research progress may involve **elevating implicit concepts into explicit architectural components**. Ideas appear first as language choices — word selection, phrasing, qualifiers — before the framework is mature enough to recognize them as structural.

This means:
1. The archive is more valuable than it appears — it contains signals that weren't legible at the time of writing
2. Reviewing old language with new eyes is a productive research activity
3. There are likely 2-3 more concepts currently sitting as nouns in the seed material that will later become mechanisms

## The Pattern

```
Concept appears as adjective/descriptor
    ↓
Framework evolves
    ↓
Adjective recognized as mechanism
    ↓
Mechanism elevated to architectural component
```

This is analogous to:
- Customers telling you the answer on day one, but you don't understand the significance yet
- A product requirement that looks like a feature request but is actually an architecture shift
