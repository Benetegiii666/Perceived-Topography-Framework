# DISCOVERY_004: Perception as Behavioral Lever

**Status:** Open — central to the framework
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## Observation

Perception may be a stronger behavioral lever than objective specification. Telling an agent what to do (direct instruction) is less effective than shaping what the agent perceives about its environment.

## Implication

This reframes intervention design. Instead of asking "what should we tell agents to do?" the question becomes "what should agents perceive about their environment?" This applies equally to AI systems (what's in the context window), organizations (what's on the dashboard), and individuals (what information is visible).
