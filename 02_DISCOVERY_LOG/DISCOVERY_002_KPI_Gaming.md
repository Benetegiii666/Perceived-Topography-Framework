# DISCOVERY_002: KPI Gaming

**Status:** Deepened — originally reward hacking parallel, now information surface corruption
**Date:** 2026-06-09

## Original Observation

KPI gaming in organizations parallels reward hacking in AI systems. Agents optimize for the measured signal rather than the intended outcome.

## Deepened Understanding (via EC_004)

The original framing: KPI Gaming ≈ Reward Hacking.

The deeper framing:

```
KPI Gaming
    ↓
Information Surface Corruption
    ↓
Distorted Perception Construction
    ↓
Distorted Behavior
```

KPIs are **information surfaces** — mechanisms by which organizational reality becomes available to perception. When an agent games a KPI, they are not changing reality. They are **corrupting the information surface**, which distorts the perception construction process for every agent downstream.

This reframing matters because it changes the intervention:

| Framing | Intervention |
|---------|-------------|
| "Bad incentives" | Redesign the reward |
| "Information surface corruption" | Redesign the surface, or add redundant surfaces |

The second intervention is different and arguably more actionable. You don't just ask "are we rewarding the right thing?" You ask "are we exposing a faithful information surface?"

## The "So What?" Test

Two genuinely different diagnostic questions emerge:

1. **Are we optimizing the wrong incentives?** (traditional framing)
2. **Are we exposing a distorted information surface?** (framework framing)

These lead to different interventions. The first redesigns rewards. The second redesigns the interface between reality and perception — adding redundant surfaces, improving surface fidelity, or detecting surface corruption.
