# DISCOVERY_013: Information Ecosystem Mapping Precedes Context Construction

**Status:** Candidate — requires additional operational examples
**Date:** 2026-06-10

## Observation

When asked how to design a Marketing Context Layer, the first instinct was not campaign workflow or decision support. The first instinct was to map the information ecosystem:

- Artifacts
- Formats
- Storage
- Authorship
- Sharing patterns
- Governance

This suggests that operational context-layer development may begin with **topography mapping before decision construction**.

## Implication

Provides concrete support for EC_005 (Topography Mapping vs Navigation). Mapping activities appear operationally distinct from navigation activities — you need to understand the landscape before you can design systems that navigate it.

## Pressure Test

Two independence questions:

1. **Can an organization improve mapping quality without improving navigation quality?** (e.g., better audits and inventories, but no change to decision support tools)
2. **Can navigation quality improve without improved mapping?** (e.g., better dashboards built on incomplete understanding of what data exists)

If both are possible, mapping and navigation are separable — and EC_005 gains support for the distinction.

If navigation always improves when mapping improves (and vice versa), they may be too tightly coupled to justify separate treatment.
