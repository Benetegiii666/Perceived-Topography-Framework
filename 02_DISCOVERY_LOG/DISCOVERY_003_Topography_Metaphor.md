# DISCOVERY_003: Topography Metaphor

**Status:** Open — under examination by FL_002
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## Observation

The concept of topography emerged from a marble-on-a-landscape metaphor. A marble placed on a surface with slopes, valleys, and ridges will follow the gradients — not because it "decides" to, but because the landscape makes certain paths more probable than others.

## Implication

This metaphor became the foundation for the framework's central concept: that environmental structure (topography) creates gradients that shape behavior. The metaphor works across domains — agents in organizations, AI systems navigating context, humans making decisions within institutional structures.

## Open Question

FL_002 asks whether topography is fundamental (a real structural feature of environments) or derived (a useful metaphor that could be restated in terms of incentives, constraints, or fields). The answer matters: if topography is just a metaphor, it may not survive the "so what?" test against existing frameworks.
