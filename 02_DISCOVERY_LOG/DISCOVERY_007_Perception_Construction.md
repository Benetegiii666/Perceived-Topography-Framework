# DISCOVERY_007: Perception Construction

**Status:** Open — actively evolving
**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material, deepened in current session)

## Observation

Perception is not passive reception of environmental information. It is an active construction process. Agents build their model of reality through retrieval, filtering, weighting, and assembly of available information.

## Implication

The shift from "perception" to "perception construction" is significant because:
1. It introduces a mechanism (how does the agent build its view?)
2. It creates the opening for Information Surfaces (EC_004) — the materials from which perception is constructed
3. It explains why two agents in the same environment can perceive different landscapes

## Evolution

- **Seed material:** Perception defined as "the optimizer's constructed model of reality" — the word "constructed" was already present
- **Current session:** FL_006 and FL_007 elevated "construction" from an adjective to a core mechanism
- **Emerging:** EC_003 and EC_004 are developing the retrieval/information surface substrate for construction
