**Subject:** Campaign brief workflow discovery

Hi [Name],

I'm working through a small prototype idea around how campaign briefs come together and how teams preserve the reasoning behind campaign decisions.

The basic idea is this: a lot of useful campaign reasoning lives in people's heads, meeting notes, prior experience, informal feedback, or scattered documents. When the next campaign starts, teams often have to rediscover the same assumptions, claim boundaries, audience logic, or lessons from prior campaigns.

I'm not looking to evaluate the team or critique the current process. I'm trying to understand how the work actually happens so we can explore whether a lightweight system could help preserve the reasoning behind campaign briefs from one cycle to the next.

I'll share a short executive summary before we meet so you have the context for where these questions are coming from. The deeper paper exists too, but the summary should be enough for this conversation.

For the session, I'd like to do one of two things:

1. Walk through a recent campaign and understand how the brief came together, what decisions were made, what information was used, and what was learned afterward.

2. Walk through the opening of an upcoming campaign and understand how the team frames the goal, audience, message, sources, claims, and review process.

The goal is to understand the workflow, the artifacts, and the decision points — not to judge the work.
