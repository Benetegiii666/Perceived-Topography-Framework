# Jeremy Marketing Strategy Meeting Guide

**Meeting type:** Pre-start reconnaissance
**Goal:** Get informed, not pitch a finished solution.
**Note:** Record the meeting. The transcript will be processed into discovery forms.

---

## Goal Of The Meeting

Use this meeting to understand:

- How marketing strategy currently thinks about campaign briefs
- Whether campaign brief creation is a meaningful first workflow
- What artifacts already exist
- What decisions are most strategy-heavy
- Where reasoning gets lost between campaigns
- Who else should eventually be involved

---

## Suggested Opening

"I've been working on a way to think about AI support for marketing that is less about generating content from scratch and more about preserving the reasoning behind campaign decisions.

My working theory is that teams often preserve the final artifact — like a campaign brief — but lose some of the reasoning that produced it: audience assumptions, claim boundaries, source material, tradeoffs, and lessons from prior campaigns.

I'm not looking to evaluate the team or prescribe a system. I'm trying to understand whether campaign brief creation is the right workflow to study first, and what would make this useful rather than burdensome."

---

## Questions To Ask Jeremy

### Workflow and Decision Structure

1. When a campaign brief is created, where does the real strategy work happen?

2. What parts of the brief tend to get re-litigated or re-explained from campaign to campaign?

3. Which campaign decisions are most dependent on experience or judgment?

### Sources and Artifacts

4. What sources does the team usually rely on for:
   - ICP / audience definition
   - Messaging / positioning
   - Brand voice
   - Approved claims / evidence
   - Competitive positioning
   - Prior campaign performance
   - Sales feedback

### Reasoning and Learning

5. Where does the team lose context between campaigns?

6. What lessons from past campaigns do people wish carried forward better?

### Discovery Case Candidates

7. Is there a recent campaign that would be safe and useful to walk through as an example?

8. Is there an upcoming campaign that would be useful to observe from the beginning?

### People and Authority

9. Who owns or influences:
   - Campaign strategy
   - Messaging
   - Brand voice
   - Claims / evidence
   - Performance reporting
   - Sales feedback

### Value and Risk

10. What would make an AI-assisted campaign brief workflow helpful?

11. What would make it annoying, risky, or politically sensitive?

12. Who should Benet speak with after July 13?

---

## What Not To Ask Yet

Do not ask Jeremy to:

- Approve the architecture
- Review the HLD
- Commit the team to using a prototype
- Answer technical infrastructure questions
- Define schemas
- Select a technology stack

---

## Desired Outputs

After the meeting, Benet should know:

- Whether campaign briefs are the right first wedge
- Whether recent-campaign or upcoming-campaign discovery is better
- Which campaign might be the first case
- Which artifacts to request later
- Which stakeholders to involve after July 13
- What risks or sensitivities to respect
