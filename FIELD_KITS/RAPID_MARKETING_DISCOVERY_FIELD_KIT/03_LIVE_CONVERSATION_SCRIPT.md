# Live Conversation Script

*Use this to open the stakeholder meeting. Read or paraphrase.*

---

## Opening

I want to frame this before we jump in.

I have been working on a philosophy and architecture around how teams make decisions with AI and knowledge systems. The short version is that I think teams often preserve the final artifact — like the campaign brief — but lose the reasoning that created it.

So what I am interested in is not just "what does the brief say?" but things like:

- What was the goal?
- What assumptions were active?
- What sources did you trust?
- What claims were allowed or not allowed?
- What made the brief feel good enough to move forward?
- What did the campaign later teach you?
- Did that learning actually carry into the next campaign?

I'm not evaluating the team or the process. I'm trying to learn how the work really happens so we can see whether a lightweight prototype could help preserve useful reasoning from one campaign cycle to the next.

We can do this either by walking through a recent campaign or by walking through an upcoming campaign that is just opening.

---

## Plain Language Vocabulary

If any of these terms come up during the conversation:

| Term | What I mean |
|---|---|
| Decision artifact | The thing produced — like the campaign brief |
| Information surface | A source the team checks — brand voice, ICP, claims list, sales feedback |
| Assumption | Something the team believes while making the brief, whether or not it has been tested |
| Sufficiency | Why the team decided it had enough to move forward |
| Learning | What the outcome taught the team |
| Reuse | Whether that learning shapes the next campaign |
| Governance | Rules about what claims can be made, what requires approval, what evidence is needed |

---

## Transition to Questions

Great, so let's pick one of two options:

**Option A:** Walk me through a recent campaign — one that has already launched or completed. I'll ask about how the brief came together, what decisions were made, and what the outcome taught you.

**Option B:** Walk me through an upcoming campaign — one that is just starting. I'll ask about how you're framing the goal, audience, message, and what sources you'll check.

Which works better for you?
