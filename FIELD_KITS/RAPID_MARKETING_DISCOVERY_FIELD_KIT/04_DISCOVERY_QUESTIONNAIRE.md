# Discovery Questionnaire — Campaign Brief Workflow

*Source: adapted from `IMPLEMENTATION_PLANNING/CAMPAIGN_BRIEF_WORKFLOW_DISCOVERY_v0.1.md` and `COMPANION_ARTIFACTS/BUILD_DISCOVERY_GUIDE_v1.0.md`, Categories 1-3.*

*This is the conversational version for live stakeholder use. For the full internal question bank (all 13 categories), see `04A_FULL_QUESTION_BANK_INTERNAL.md`.*

---

## Track A — Recent Campaign Review

*Use when they have a campaign that recently launched or completed.*

### 1. Campaign Context

- What campaign are we discussing?
- What was it for? (product, audience, vertical)
- When did it run?
- How did it perform overall?

### 2. Workflow Boundary

- What triggered this campaign? (calendar cycle, stakeholder request, product launch, market event?)
- Who requested the brief?
- Who wrote it?
- Who reviewed or approved it?
- What did the final brief look like? (document, template, deck, other?)
- When was the brief considered "done"?

### 3. Core Decision

- What was the core goal of this campaign? (awareness, pipeline, education, partner enablement?)
- Was there a secondary goal?
- Who was the target audience?
- How was the audience chosen?
- What messaging angle was selected? Why that angle?

### 4. Brief Structure

- What sections were in the brief?
- Which sections were written from scratch vs. copied from a prior campaign?
- Which sections were the strongest? Which were the weakest?
- Were there any sections that were vague but still treated as good enough?

### 5. Information Sources

- What sources did you actually check while creating this brief?
- Which sources were the most useful?
- Which sources were outdated or hard to find?
- Were there sources you wished existed but didn't?
- Did you ask anyone directly for information that wasn't documented?

### 6. Claims and Governance

- Did the brief make any claims that required evidence or approval?
- Were there claims you considered but decided against? Why?
- Were there brand or compliance rules that shaped the messaging?
- Did anyone review the claims before launch?

### 7. Assumptions

- What were the main assumptions behind the brief? (about the audience, the message, the timing, the competition)
- Were any of those assumptions tested or just accepted?
- Looking back, were any of those assumptions wrong or incomplete?

### 8. Sufficiency

- What made the brief feel "good enough" to move forward?
- Was there anything you still weren't sure about when you approved it?
- If you could have investigated one more thing before launch, what would it have been?

### 9. Outcome

- What results did the campaign produce?
- Were the results what you expected?
- What surprised you?
- Did any of the results contradict your assumptions?

### 10. Learning

- What did you learn from this campaign?
- Did that learning change how you approached the next campaign?
- If someone started a similar campaign tomorrow, what would you tell them?
- Is that advice written down anywhere, or does it live in your head?

## Track B — Upcoming Campaign Opening

*Use when they have a campaign that is just starting.*

### 1. Campaign Setup

- What campaign is opening?
- What triggered it?
- Who is requesting it?
- What's the timeline?

### 2. What the Brief Needs to Decide

- What is the goal of this campaign?
- Who is the target audience?
- What messaging angle are you considering?
- What will the final brief look like?

### 3. Starting Assumptions

- What are you assuming about the audience right now?
- What are you assuming about the message?
- How confident are you in those assumptions?
- What would change your approach?

### 4. Sources to Check

- What sources will you check before writing the brief?
- Where do those sources live?
- Who owns them?
- Are they current?
- Are any critical sources missing?

### 5. Claims and Boundaries

- Will the brief make any claims that require evidence or approval?
- Are there claims you know you can't make? Why?
- What brand or compliance rules apply?

### 6. Review and Approval

- Who will review the brief?
- What will they check?
- Is there a formal approval step?
- What happens if the reviewer has concerns?

### 7. What Would Make the Brief Sufficient

- What would make you feel confident enough to proceed?
- What information would you want before finalizing?
- What would make you pause and investigate more?

### 8. Outcome Expectation

- What results do you expect this campaign to produce?
- How will you measure success?
- When will the outcome data be available?
- What would a disappointing result tell you?

## Quick Close-Out Questions

*Use these at the end of either track.*

- What part of this process is most dependent on someone's memory or judgment?
- What do people often have to re-explain from campaign to campaign?
- What do new people struggle to know?
- What information do you wish surfaced automatically when starting a brief?
- What lesson from a past campaign should never be forgotten?
- What is one thing the prototype should absolutely not do?
