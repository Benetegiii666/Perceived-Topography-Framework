# AHA_006: Governance Shapes Terrain, Perception Determines Visibility, Optimization Chooses Path

**Date:** 2026-06-09 (from seed material)

## The Moment

> "Governance shapes the terrain. Perception determines which terrain is visible. Optimization chooses the path."

A three-part articulation of the causal chain. Each layer has a distinct role: governance is the designer, perception is the filter, optimization is the navigator.

## Why It Matters

This separates three intervention points that are often conflated:
1. **Governance** — reshape the terrain itself (structural intervention)
2. **Perception design** — change what part of the terrain is visible (information intervention)
3. **Optimization constraints** — change how the agent navigates what it perceives (behavioral intervention)

Most organizations only use #1 and #3. The framework argues #2 may be the most powerful.
