# AHA_009: Visibility Is Not Attention

**Status:** Candidate — not yet promoted
**Date:** 2026-06-09

## The Moment

The magician example. The cleanest independence test we've encountered.

The coin is visible. The audience is capable of seeing it. Yet they do not perceive what happened. Because attention was directed elsewhere.

**Visibility** answers: "What can be seen?"
**Attention** answers: "What receives resources?"

## Why It Matters

This distinction immediately felt more concrete than many earlier discussions. It passed the independence test on contact — same visibility, different attention, different perception. If this holds, the space between information surfaces and perception construction may contain a distinct mechanism.
