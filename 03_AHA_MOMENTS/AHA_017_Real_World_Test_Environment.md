# AHA_017: The Framework Has Entered a Real-World Test Environment

**Status:** Retired from paper scope — project-specific, preserved in repo
**Date:** 2026-06-10

## The Moment

A potential role focused on marketing context-layer construction and governance creates an opportunity to evaluate framework concepts against operational reality rather than theoretical examples.

## Why It Matters

Questions previously explored as conceptual distinctions — Goal, Context, Attention, Interpretation — may become **actual governance responsibilities and failure-analysis categories**. The framework would no longer be evaluated against thought experiments. It would be evaluated against whether it helps someone do their job better.

## Pressure Test Opportunity

Evaluate whether D012's failure classifications improve:
- Incident diagnosis
- Context-layer governance
- Retrieval design
- Stakeholder interviews
- Campaign review processes

## What This Changes

The framework has been building toward testability (AHA_013, D011, D012). A real-world application environment would provide:
1. **Evidence for or against the diagnostic model** (AHA_016) — does classifying failures by type actually improve investigation?
2. **Natural experiments** — same team, different context layers, measurable outcome differences
3. **Stakeholder feedback** — do the categories resonate with practitioners, or do they feel forced?
4. **Falsification opportunities** — failures that don't fit the categories, interventions that don't match predictions
