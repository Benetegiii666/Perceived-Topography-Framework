# AHA_016: Diagnostic Categories Are More Valuable Than Corrective Actions

**Status:** Candidate — not yet promoted
**Date:** 2026-06-10

## The Moment

The framework's categories derive value not because they prescribe fixes, but because they **constrain investigation into different channels of evidence**.

The shift:

> From: "What is the fix?"
> To: "What kind of failure is this?"

## Why It Matters

This reframes the framework's primary value. It may function more effectively as a **diagnostic model** than as a prescriptive model.

A prescriptive model says: "Do X to fix Y."
A diagnostic model says: "The failure is in layer Z — now investigate Z."

The second is more robust because:
1. It doesn't require the framework to know the specific fix — only to narrow the search
2. It works across domains without domain-specific prescriptions
3. It reduces the cost of misdiagnosis by channeling investigation before intervention
4. It connects directly to D011 — if the diagnostic category is correct, targeted investigation should outperform unfocused investigation

This may also address Open Question #4 (descriptive vs prescriptive). The answer might be: **diagnostic** — a third option that sits between description and prescription.
