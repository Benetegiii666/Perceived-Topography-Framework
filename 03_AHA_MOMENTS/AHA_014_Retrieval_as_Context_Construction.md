# AHA_014: Retrieval May Be a Context Construction Mechanism

**Status:** Candidate — not yet promoted
**Date:** 2026-06-10

## The Moment

Retrieval has been treated primarily as a visibility mechanism — it makes gradients visible that weren't before. But many advanced retrieval patterns appear to function by supplying **context that shapes interpretation** rather than merely exposing new information.

Two decision-makers observe the same visible gradient but arrive at different conclusions because they possess different contextual knowledge. The difference is not visibility. The difference is context.

## Examples

- Agentic Retrieval
- Concept-Based Retrieval
- Goal-Driven Gradient Selection
- Memory Systems
- Transfer Documents
- Knowledge Platforms

## Why It Matters

If retrieval influences behavior through context construction — not solely through visibility enhancement — then the 5-layer chain may need refinement. The transformation from visible gradient to perceived gradient is not just "interpretation." It is interpretation *shaped by retrieved context*.

This reframes retrieval system design: the goal is not just "surface relevant information" but "construct the context that enables correct interpretation of visible gradients."
