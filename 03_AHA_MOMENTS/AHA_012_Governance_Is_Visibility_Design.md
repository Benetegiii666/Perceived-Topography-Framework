# AHA_012: Governance May Be Visibility Design

**Status:** Candidate — not yet promoted
**Date:** 2026-06-09

## The Moment

The progression:

1. Governance controls behavior.
2. Governance shapes topography.
3. Governance shapes visibility.
4. Governance may often be the act of ensuring important gradients become visible.

## Why It Matters

This reframed the governance conversation productively. Instead of asking "how do we control what agents do?" the question becomes "how do we ensure agents can see what matters?"

Not proven — but it shifts governance from a control mechanism to a visibility mechanism. That has different design implications: instead of writing more rules, you design better information surfaces.
