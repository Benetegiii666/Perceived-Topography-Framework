# Supporting Theses

Secondary claims that extend the core thesis. Each must be independently defensible.

## ST-1: Topography Over Incentives

Topography exerts greater influence on behavior than incentives alone. Incentives are one component of topography, not the whole of it. Structure, visibility, constraints, affordances, and costs all shape the gradient landscape.

## ST-2: Perception Mediates

Perception mediates the relationship between topography and behavior. Agents do not respond to objective topography — they respond to their *perceived* topography. Two agents in the same environment may perceive different landscapes and therefore exhibit different behaviors.

## ST-3: Governance as Environmental Design

Governance functions primarily through environmental design rather than direct behavioral control. Effective governance reshapes the topography; ineffective governance attempts to override it through rules and mandates.

## ST-4: Alignment as Environment Design

AI alignment concerns can be reframed as a special case of designing environments for optimization. The alignment problem is not fundamentally about constraining the agent — it is about shaping the landscape the agent optimizes within.
