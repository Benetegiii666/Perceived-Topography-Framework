# Core Thesis

> All other files in the repository exist to support, challenge, or refine this.

## Statement

**Behavior emerges from the interaction between optimizers and perceived topography.**

Behavior is not primarily a product of individual choice or incentive design. It emerges from the perceived topography of the environment — the landscape of possibilities, constraints, and gradients that agents perceive around them.

## Origin

This thesis evolved from the seed observation (Aha 001):

> "As agent ecosystems become more sophisticated, the challenge may become less about rewarding agents and more about shaping the landscape they optimize within."

## Implication

If this is true, then the primary lever for shaping behavior is not reward design or direct instruction — it is environmental design. The question shifts from "how do we tell agents what to do?" to "what landscape are we placing agents within?"

## Status

Stable since v0.1. Under pressure from FL_007 (Information Surfaces may add a layer between reality and perception) but the core claim — behavior emerges from optimizer-topography interaction — has held.
