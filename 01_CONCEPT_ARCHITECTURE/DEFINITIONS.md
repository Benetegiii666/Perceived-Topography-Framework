# Definitions

Precise definitions of all framework terms. Definition changes should be classified and logged before being applied here.

## Core Terms

**Reality**
The actual state of the world, independent of observation.

**Topography**
The structure of gradients, affordances, constraints, costs, permissions, incentives, and visibility through which behavior becomes more or less likely to emerge.

**Perception**
The optimizer's constructed model of reality and the topography visible from its position.

> *Note:* FL_003 questions whether perception is a layer or a mechanism. FL_006/FL_007 suggest perception may be actively *constructed* through information surfaces rather than passively received. The definition above is v0.1 and may evolve.

**Optimization**
The process by which a system responds to perceived gradients in pursuit of an objective.

**Behavior**
The observable pattern that emerges from the interaction between an optimizer and its perceived topography.

**Outcomes**
Changes in reality produced by behavior.

## Candidate Terms (Not Yet Promoted)

**Information Surfaces** *(EC_004)*
The mechanisms by which reality becomes available to perception. Neither agent nor environment — the interface through which topography becomes perceivable. Examples: eyes, RAG pipelines, KPI dashboards, media.

**Perception Construction** *(FL_006, FL_007)*
The active process by which an agent builds its perceived model of topography from available information surfaces. Distinct from passive "perception" in that it implies agency in model-building.

**Optimizer**
Any system that responds to perceived gradients — human, organizational, or artificial. Used interchangeably with "agent" in most contexts.
