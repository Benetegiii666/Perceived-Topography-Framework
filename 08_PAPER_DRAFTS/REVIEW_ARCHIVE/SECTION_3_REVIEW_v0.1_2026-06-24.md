## Notes (specific fixes for v0.2)

**1. Justify the four-part structure.** Add after the "at least four things" sentence: "These four are not exhaustive. Section 6 develops a fuller architecture with seven reasoning objects. They serve here as the minimum necessary to distinguish reasoning state from context: the frame tells us the goal and constraints, the premise stack tells us the assumptions, the sufficiency rationale tells us when investigation stopped, and the update path tells us what changed."

**2. Show reasoning-state alternative in healthcare example.** After "A context-only system may preserve the draft," add 2-3 sentences showing what reasoning-state preservation would look like: the claim is flagged because the evidence boundary is active, the sufficiency rationale records that clinical-outcome language requires approved evidence, and the distinction between operational and clinical claims is preserved for the next campaign.

**3. Add three citations.** Simon 1955 in the stopping-conditions paragraph. Weick 1995 as parenthetical for "learning becomes storytelling." Lee 1997 as one sentence acknowledging design rationale systems as prior work on preserving the "why."

**4. Name single-loop/double-loop.** In the Argyris & Schon paragraph, add the specific terms.

**5. Restructure patterned four-part list.** Vary the "First...Second...Third...Fourth..." sentence openings.

**6. Cut two setup sentences.** "Consider a healthcare marketing workflow" and "That question prepares the framework's next move."

**7. Reconcile justification vs. transition framing.** Add one sentence acknowledging that reasoning state is both a condition (what makes action justified) and a transition (how the system moves from goal through action to update).

**8. Reduce one restatement.** The context/reasoning-state distinction is stated four times. Cut the fourth ("This gives reasoning state a different role from context. Context supports the current answer. Reasoning state supports future judgment.") or merge it with the closing.
